The Company

Swann Educational Solutions offers a full range of strategic services to authorizing entities such as districts, states, university authorizers, and other organizations considering adding charter schools to their portfolio. Those services include:

  • Application Review and Evaluation
  • Review Team Training
  • District/State board training
  • Starting an effective charter school office
  • Executive leadership development and coaching
  • Strategic Planning
  • Consultation for school turnaround, transition, and restart
  • Portfolio strategy and implementation
  • Development of quality oversight and monitoring processes and procedures

Additionally, Swann Educational Solutions also offers complete consultation services to charter school developers, including:

  • Research
  • School development and writing of the initial application
  • Governance board training
  • Leadership development and coaching

The Founder

Carol Swann has over 30 years in education with extensive experience working with districts and states regarding the charter school authorization process, strategic planning, training, monitoring and oversight, and policy development for charter schools. Carol has been responsible for charter policy and procedures, writing and updating the charter application and scoring rubric in conjunction with state laws, and working with external partners such as the National Association of Charter School Authorizers (NACSA) to ensure a quality authorizing process utilizing best practices from across the country. As a direct result of this work, Carol has developed training modules to assist authorizers, providing training for review teams, school boards, university charter offices, and state charter offices.

Carol also organized the Tennessee Association of Charter School Authorizers (TACSA), and wrote the TACSA Principles and Standards and by-laws. She was recently nominated as President-elect for 2017. This organization is developing model authorizing policies and practices for authorizers in Tennessee as well as looking at model charter laws around the nation in order to inform and educate legislative work.

Additionally, Carol has served as Chief Operating Officer of a charter school and has also developed resources to assist potential charter school developers in their application processes.
